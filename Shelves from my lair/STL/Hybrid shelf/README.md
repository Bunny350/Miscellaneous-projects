# Rackmount network + filament shelf (aka, hybrid shelf)
<img alt="Rackmount network + filament shelf, dubbed the most powerful shelf ever made" src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/Bunny350/Miscellaneous-projects/main/Shelves%20from%20my%20lair/Media/most-powerful-shelf.jpg" />

This shelf, dubbed "the most powerful shelf ever made," can house an entire rackmount UPS of around 25kg while storing up to 6 - 7 spools of 1kg filament and 4 PolyDryer boxes, or 14 spools of 1kg filament. This shelf requires structs to support the weight of the UPS and the rest of loads to prevent sagging.

## Important note
Please see its CAD files on how it will be built. There are no fasteners on the file so you have to prepare yourselves the following fasteners:
* 8x M3x16mm BHCS (PolyDryer holding, with 2mm spacers)
* 10x M4x40mm SHCS (2 of them are for the front normal spool holder, the rest are rack mount base and back spool holder)
* 10x M5x10mm BHCS (2 of them are for PolyDryer holding beam, the rest are for the struct constraints)
* 2x M5x20mm SHCS (PolyDryer holding, 530mm small version)
* 2x M5x35mm SHCS (PolyDryer holding, 560mm normal version)
* 10x to 12x M6x12mm BHCS (6 of them are mounting rack ears to 560mm normal version)
* 36x M6x20mm BHCS (including struct constraints, spool beams and panel mounting)
* 2x or 6x M6x30mm BHCS (for the front struct constraints, additional 4 of them will be mounting rack mounting ears to the struct constraint pieces.)
* **(New)** 2x M6x35mm BHCS (for sag compensation pieces)
* 8x M8x20mm BHCS
* Tapping all 3030 extrusions (one-side, opposing blind holes) and 1515 & 2020 (both sides) extrusions in 50cm are required.
* All 3030 extrusions should have one **(just one)** blind hole opposing from the tapped sides.
* The back 280mm 3030 extrusions (a pair) need to have an additional one-side blind holes, one within 60mm from the top (tapped side) and one within 100mm from the bottom (blind hole).
* 6x Self-tapping screws with diameters within from 6mm to 8mm.

Avoid drilling the plastic screw plugs just because of it requiring a high force, or the shelf will sag (I tried).

**Sag compensation pieces are recommended. These are here to change the center of gravity of the shelves in an attempt to kill the sagging that will later cause the shelf to fall off. This is not an Ikea shelf, this is an Oitswilliam shelf, where super heavy things are loaded.**

For the network shelf, you may test the payload vs sag test where you must look out for any sags within 7 to 21 days of test operation. If there are sags within such time period, then you need to stow it as soon as you can, and find a more rigid way to secure.
